London's position in global commodity markets is historically anomalous and intellectually revealing.

The city that once dominated global commodity trade through the physical infrastructure of empire — colonial ports, trading houses, merchant vessels — has transformed itself into a purely abstract layer of the commodity system. The docks are gone. The warehouses are gone. What remains is something more durable and more powerful: the institutional infrastructure that determines what everything is worth.

This transformation illustrates a broader principle: **in mature systems, the power of price formation exceeds the power of physical control.**

The node that sets the price captures a share of every transaction in the system, regardless of where the physical commodity moves.
