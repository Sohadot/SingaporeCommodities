Geneva is the clearest possible evidence that modern commodity power has become dematerialized.

The city has no geographic advantage for commodity trade. Its position in Switzerland offers no natural resource base, no maritime access, no manufacturing tradition of scale. Yet the companies headquartered in Geneva collectively handle a larger share of global commodity flows than any port city on earth.

This is the final evolution of the trading house model that originated in the merchant cities of medieval Europe: **commodity power has separated entirely from physical presence and relocated to wherever institutional complexity is best managed.**

Geneva represents the invisible layer above even the coordination layer — the meta-organization of the organizational system itself.
