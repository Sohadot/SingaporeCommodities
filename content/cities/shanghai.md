Shanghai is the reason the global commodity system functions as it does.

The scale of Chinese demand — built over three decades of the most rapid industrialization in human history — has fundamentally altered the physics of global commodity flows. Trade lanes, pricing relationships, storage patterns, and routing decisions that took shape over centuries have been reorganized around the gravitational pull of Chinese consumption.

Singapore's routing function exists largely because China demands commodities. Rotterdam's storage function exists largely because Europe needs alternatives to oversupply and undersupply cycles partially driven by Chinese demand. London's Brent benchmark is watched intently in Shanghai because Chinese buyers price against it daily.

**Shanghai does not merely participate in the global commodity system. It defines its trajectory.**

The future of commodity markets — including the future relevance of every other node in this network — will be determined in significant part by decisions made in Shanghai: how aggressively China internalizes commodity pricing, how quickly it transitions its energy system, and how it structures the commodity dependencies of the Global South through its infrastructure investment programs.
